Overview

Thymic peptide bioregulator used alongside Thymosin Alpha-1 for immune modulation. Part of the Khavinson peptide bioregulator research programme.

How it works

Thymalin is a peptide bioregulator derived from the thymus gland, which plays a role in modulating the immune system. It is thought to enhance the activity of T-cells and other immune cells, thereby improving immune response. Thymalin may also influence the production of cytokines, which are crucial for cell signaling in immune responses. Additionally, it is believed to have antioxidant properties that protect cells from oxidative stress.

What the research describes

  • Enhancement of immune function
  • Reduction of inflammation
  • Improvement in wound healing
  • Potential anti-aging effects
  • Antioxidant activity

Evidence summary

There are limited human trials on Thymalin, with most studies conducted in Russia. These studies suggest potential benefits in immune modulation and anti-aging, but the sample sizes are small and methodologies vary. More rigorous, large-scale trials are needed to confirm these effects.

Evidence grade C — Early research.
