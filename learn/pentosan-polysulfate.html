Overview

Semi-synthetic polysaccharide with chondroprotective and anti-inflammatory properties. FDA-approved for interstitial cystitis. Studied for osteoarthritis.

How it works

Pentosan Polysulfate works by mimicking the structure of glycosaminoglycans, which are natural components of the extracellular matrix in cartilage. It binds to proteins and cells, reducing inflammation and preventing the breakdown of cartilage. Additionally, it inhibits enzymes that degrade cartilage and may improve blood flow to affected tissues.

What the research describes

  • Reduces inflammation in joint tissues
  • Decreases pain associated with osteoarthritis
  • Improves joint function
  • Protects cartilage from degradation
  • May enhance bladder lining in interstitial cystitis

Evidence summary

There are several human trials investigating Pentosan Polysulfate for osteoarthritis, showing moderate efficacy in reducing pain and improving joint function. However, the long-term effects and optimal dosing require further research. The evidence for its use in interstitial cystitis is more robust, with FDA approval for this condition.

Evidence grade B, Emerging.
