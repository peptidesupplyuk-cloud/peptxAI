Overview

Synthetic GnRH that stimulates pituitary release of LH and FSH, supporting natural testosterone production and fertility.

How it works

Gonadorelin is a synthetic form of gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH) that binds to GnRH receptors in the pituitary gland. This binding stimulates the release of luteinizing hormone (LH) and follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH), which are crucial for the regulation of the reproductive system. LH and FSH promote the production of sex hormones such as testosterone and estrogen, supporting fertility and sexual function.

What the research describes

  • Stimulates the release of LH and FSH from the pituitary gland
  • Supports natural testosterone production
  • Enhances fertility in both men and women
  • Regulates menstrual cycles
  • May aid in the diagnosis of pituitary disorders

Evidence summary

There are several human clinical trials that demonstrate the efficacy of gonadorelin in stimulating LH and FSH release, supporting its use in fertility treatments. However, more research is needed to fully understand its long-term safety profile and effects in diverse populations.
