Overview

Synthetic copper tripeptide analog of GHK-Cu. Stimulates collagen production, improves skin elasticity, and supports wound healing with enhanced stability over its parent compound.

How it works

AHK-Cu is a copper tripeptide that binds to copper ions, facilitating their transport into cells. This peptide enhances the activity of enzymes involved in collagen synthesis and tissue repair. It also modulates the expression of genes related to skin regeneration and anti-inflammatory responses. AHK-Cu is more stable than its parent compound, GHK-Cu, allowing for prolonged activity in biological systems.

What the research describes

  • Stimulates collagen production
  • Improves skin elasticity
  • Enhances wound healing
  • Reduces inflammation
  • Promotes hair growth

Use areas

Evidence summary

There are no human clinical trials specifically evaluating AHK-Cu. Most evidence comes from in vitro studies and animal models demonstrating its effects on skin and hair. The lack of human data limits the ability to fully understand its efficacy and safety profile.

Evidence grade C, Early research.
