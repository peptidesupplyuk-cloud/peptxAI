Overview

Cell-penetrating peptide that disrupts the FOXO4-p53 interaction, selectively triggering apoptosis in senescent cells. Experimental senolytic agent.

How it works

FOXO4-DRI is a peptide designed to interfere with the interaction between FOXO4 and p53 proteins. By disrupting this interaction, it selectively induces apoptosis in senescent cells, which are cells that have stopped dividing and contribute to aging and age-related diseases. This targeted action helps to clear these dysfunctional cells from tissues, potentially improving tissue function and health.

What the research describes

  • Induces apoptosis in senescent cells
  • Reduces markers of cellular senescence
  • Improves tissue function in aged tissues
  • Potentially enhances physical function in aged organisms
  • May reduce inflammation associated with senescent cells

Use areas

FOXO4-DRI is researched across multiple areas:

Longevity

FOXO4-DRI is relevant to longevity as it targets and removes senescent cells, which are implicated in aging and age-related diseases.

Health optimisation

FOXO4-DRI may contribute to health optimisation by reducing the burden of senescent cells, potentially improving overall tissue health and function.

Evidence summary

There are currently no published human clinical trials for FOXO4-DRI. Most evidence comes from animal studies and in vitro experiments, which show promising senolytic effects. The lack of human data represents a significant gap in understanding its safety and efficacy in humans.

Evidence grade C, Early research.
