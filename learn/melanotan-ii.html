Overview

Synthetic peptide that stimulates melanin production. Also has appetite suppression and libido effects.

How it works

Melanotan II is a synthetic analogue of the alpha-melanocyte-stimulating hormone (α-MSH), which binds to melanocortin receptors, particularly MC1R, to stimulate melanin production in the skin. This results in increased pigmentation. It also affects MC4R, which is involved in appetite regulation and sexual function, leading to its effects on appetite suppression and libido enhancement.

What the research describes

  • Increased skin pigmentation
  • Appetite suppression
  • Enhanced libido
  • Potential weight loss
  • Possible reduction in UV-induced skin damage

Evidence summary

There are limited human trials on Melanotan II, primarily focusing on its effects on skin pigmentation and libido. These studies show increased pigmentation and libido, but the sample sizes are small and long-term safety data is lacking.

Evidence grade C, Early research.
