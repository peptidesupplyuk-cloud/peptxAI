Overview

Second-generation GHRP that triggers significant GH release. More selective than GHRP-6 with moderate appetite stimulation.

How it works

GHRP-2 works by binding to the growth hormone secretagogue receptor (GHS-R) on pituitary cells, stimulating the release of growth hormone (GH). It mimics ghrelin, a natural hormone that regulates appetite and energy balance, thereby increasing GH levels. This peptide also influences the hypothalamus to enhance GH secretion.

What the research describes

  • Increased growth hormone secretion
  • Moderate appetite stimulation
  • Improved muscle mass
  • Enhanced recovery from exercise
  • Potential fat loss

Use areas

GHRP-2 is researched across multiple areas:

Longevity

GHRP-2 may support longevity by promoting GH release, which is associated with improved body composition and metabolic health.

Fitness

GHRP-2 is relevant for athletes seeking enhanced recovery and muscle growth due to its GH-boosting effects.

Health optimisation

GHRP-2 may aid in general health optimisation by improving body composition and metabolic function through increased GH levels.

Evidence summary

There are limited human trials on GHRP-2. Some studies suggest it increases GH levels and may improve body composition, but comprehensive clinical trials are lacking. More research is needed to confirm its efficacy and safety in humans.

Evidence grade C — Early research.
