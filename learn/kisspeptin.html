Overview

Master regulator of reproductive hormones. Stimulates GnRH release to support LH, FSH, and testosterone production.

How it works

Kisspeptin binds to the GPR54 receptor on neurons in the hypothalamus, stimulating the release of gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH). This action triggers the pituitary gland to secrete luteinizing hormone (LH) and follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH), which are crucial for reproductive function. Kisspeptin also influences the production of sex hormones like testosterone and estradiol.

What the research describes

  • Increases secretion of GnRH
  • Enhances LH and FSH production
  • Stimulates testosterone production
  • Regulates menstrual cycle
  • Improves fertility outcomes

Evidence summary

There are several human trials indicating that kisspeptin can effectively stimulate reproductive hormone release. These studies have shown promise in treating infertility and reproductive disorders. However, more extensive trials are needed to fully understand long-term effects and optimal dosing strategies.
