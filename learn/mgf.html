Overview

Splice variant of IGF-1 released in response to mechanical stress on muscle tissue. Promotes satellite cell activation and muscle repair.

How it works

MGF is a splice variant of IGF-1 that is produced in response to mechanical overload in muscle tissue. It activates satellite cells, which are essential for muscle repair and growth. MGF also promotes protein synthesis and inhibits protein degradation, facilitating muscle hypertrophy. It may enhance local muscle regeneration by increasing the proliferation of muscle progenitor cells.

What the research describes

  • Enhances muscle repair and regeneration
  • Increases satellite cell activation
  • Promotes muscle hypertrophy
  • Facilitates protein synthesis
  • Reduces muscle protein degradation

Use areas

MGF is researched across multiple areas:

Fitness

MGF is relevant for enhancing muscle recovery and growth, potentially benefiting athletic performance.

Health optimisation

MGF may support muscle maintenance and repair, contributing to overall physical health.

Evidence summary

There are no human clinical trials specifically investigating MGF. Most evidence comes from animal studies and in vitro experiments, which suggest potential benefits for muscle repair and growth. The lack of human data and clinical trials is a significant gap.

Evidence grade C, Early research.
