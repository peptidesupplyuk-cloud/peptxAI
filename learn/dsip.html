Overview

Naturally occurring 9-amino acid neuropeptide that enhances deep (delta wave) sleep and supports circadian rhythm regulation.

How it works

Delta sleep-inducing peptide (DSIP) is thought to interact with the central nervous system by modulating the activity of neurotransmitters such as serotonin and norepinephrine. It may influence the sleep-wake cycle by promoting delta wave sleep, which is crucial for restorative sleep. DSIP is also believed to have a role in regulating the body's stress response by affecting the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis.

What the research describes

  • Enhances deep sleep quality
  • Supports circadian rhythm regulation
  • May reduce stress levels
  • Potentially modulates pain perception
  • Could improve mood stability

Use areas

DSIP is researched across multiple areas:

Longevity

DSIP may support longevity by improving sleep quality, which is linked to better overall health.

Fitness

Improved sleep from DSIP could enhance recovery and performance in athletes.

Health optimisation

DSIP may help optimise health by regulating sleep and reducing stress.

Evidence summary

There are limited human trials on DSIP, with most studies being small and preliminary. Some trials suggest improvements in sleep quality and stress reduction, but results are inconsistent. More robust, large-scale studies are needed to confirm these effects.

Evidence grade C, Early research.
