Overview

FDA-approved GHRH analog specifically effective at reducing visceral fat. One of few peptides with robust clinical trial data.

How it works

Tesamorelin is a synthetic analog of growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH), which stimulates the pituitary gland to release growth hormone. This increase in growth hormone levels leads to enhanced lipolysis, particularly in visceral fat. The peptide also influences insulin-like growth factor 1 (IGF-1) levels, which plays a role in metabolic processes.

What the research describes

  • Reduction of visceral adipose tissue
  • Increase in IGF-1 levels
  • Improvement in lipid profiles
  • Potential improvement in cognitive function
  • Enhanced lipolysis

Use areas

Tesamorelin is researched across multiple areas:

Longevity

Tesamorelin may have potential anti-ageing benefits due to its effects on growth hormone and IGF-1 levels.

Fitness

Tesamorelin can aid in reducing visceral fat, which may be beneficial for athletes looking to optimize body composition.

Health optimisation

Tesamorelin's ability to reduce visceral fat and improve metabolic markers makes it relevant for general health optimisation.

Evidence summary

Multiple human trials have demonstrated Tesamorelin's efficacy in reducing visceral fat, particularly in HIV-associated lipodystrophy. These studies consistently show significant reductions in visceral adipose tissue and improvements in metabolic markers. However, long-term safety data and effects on non-HIV populations are less well-documented.

Evidence grade A — Clinical.
