Overview

Synthetic 83-amino acid IGF-1 analog with extended half-life (20-30 hours) and enhanced potency. Stimulates hyperplasia.

How it works

IGF-1 LR3 binds to the IGF-1 receptor with high affinity, activating intracellular signaling pathways such as the PI3K/Akt pathway. This leads to increased protein synthesis and cell proliferation, particularly in muscle and bone tissues. The extended half-life allows for prolonged receptor activation compared to native IGF-1.

What the research describes

  • Increased muscle growth
  • Enhanced recovery from exercise
  • Improved bone density
  • Potential reduction in body fat
  • Increased cellular proliferation

Use areas

IGF-1 LR3 is researched across multiple areas:

Longevity

Potentially relevant due to its role in cellular proliferation and repair, but long-term safety is unproven.

Fitness

Highly relevant for muscle growth and recovery, making it popular in athletic circles.

Health optimisation

May support muscle maintenance and recovery, but safety and efficacy in non-athletic populations are not well-established.

Evidence summary

There are limited human trials specifically on IGF-1 LR3. Most evidence comes from animal studies and extrapolation from IGF-1 research. Human trials are needed to confirm efficacy and safety, particularly long-term effects.

Evidence grade C, Early research.
