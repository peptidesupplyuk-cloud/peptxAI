Overview

28-amino acid regulatory peptide involved in circadian rhythm, immune regulation, and neuroprotection. Studied for chronic inflammatory response syndrome (CIRS).

How it works

Vasoactive intestinal peptide (VIP) works by binding to specific receptors on cell surfaces, leading to the activation of adenylate cyclase and increased cyclic AMP levels. This cascade results in smooth muscle relaxation, vasodilation, and modulation of immune responses. VIP also influences circadian rhythms by acting on the suprachiasmatic nucleus in the brain.

What the research describes

  • Improves circadian rhythm regulation
  • Reduces inflammation in chronic inflammatory response syndrome (CIRS)
  • Enhances neuroprotection and cognitive function
  • Promotes vasodilation and improves blood flow
  • Modulates immune system activity

Use areas

VIP is researched across multiple areas:

Longevity

VIP's neuroprotective and anti-inflammatory effects may contribute to healthy ageing.

Health optimisation

VIP's role in circadian rhythm regulation and immune modulation supports overall health optimisation.

Evidence summary

There are a limited number of human trials investigating VIP, primarily focusing on its effects in CIRS and neuroprotection. These studies suggest potential benefits, but larger, more comprehensive trials are needed to confirm efficacy and safety. Current gaps include long-term safety data and broader population studies.

Evidence grade B, Emerging.
