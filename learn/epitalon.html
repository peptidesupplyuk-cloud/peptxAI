Overview

Telomerase-activating peptide researched for effects on cellular aging, circadian rhythm, and immune function.

How it works

Epitalon is believed to work by activating telomerase, an enzyme that adds protective caps called telomeres to the ends of chromosomes, which may slow down cellular aging. It may also influence the pineal gland to regulate melatonin production, impacting circadian rhythms. Additionally, it might modulate immune function by affecting the thymus gland.

What the research describes

  • Potential increase in telomere length
  • Improvement in sleep quality
  • Modulation of immune function
  • Possible reduction in oxidative stress
  • Regulation of circadian rhythms

Use areas

Epitalon is researched across multiple areas:

Longevity

Epitalon is relevant to longevity due to its potential effects on telomere length and cellular aging.

Health optimisation

Epitalon may support general health optimisation by potentially improving sleep and modulating immune function.

Evidence summary

There are a few small human trials suggesting potential benefits of Epitalon on telomere length and sleep quality. However, these studies are limited in size and scope, and more robust clinical trials are needed to confirm these effects.

Evidence grade C, Early research.
