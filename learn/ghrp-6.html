Overview

First-generation GHRP that stimulates GH release while activating ghrelin receptors, increasing appetite significantly.

How it works

GHRP-6 works by binding to the growth hormone secretagogue receptor (GHS-R), which stimulates the pituitary gland to release growth hormone (GH). It also activates ghrelin receptors, which are involved in regulating appetite and energy balance. This dual action increases GH levels and appetite, potentially influencing body composition and metabolism.

What the research describes

  • Increases growth hormone secretion
  • Stimulates appetite
  • May improve muscle mass
  • Potentially enhances fat metabolism
  • Could support recovery from exercise

Use areas

GHRP-6 is researched across multiple areas:

Longevity

GHRP-6 may have potential in anti-ageing by increasing growth hormone levels, which decline with age.

Fitness

GHRP-6 could aid in muscle growth and recovery, making it relevant for athletic performance.

Health optimisation

By increasing growth hormone and appetite, GHRP-6 might support overall metabolic health and body composition.

Evidence summary

There are limited human trials on GHRP-6. Some studies suggest it can increase GH levels and appetite, but comprehensive clinical data on long-term effects and safety are lacking. More robust human trials are needed to confirm these findings.

Evidence grade C — Early research.
