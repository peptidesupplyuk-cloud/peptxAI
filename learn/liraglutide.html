Overview

FDA-approved GLP-1 analog with shorter half-life than semaglutide. Requires daily dosing. Proven efficacy for weight management and type 2 diabetes.

How it works

Liraglutide is a glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonist. It mimics the action of the natural hormone GLP-1, which increases insulin secretion in response to meals, decreases glucagon release, and slows gastric emptying. This leads to improved blood glucose control and reduced appetite.

What the research describes

  • Improves glycemic control in type 2 diabetes
  • Promotes weight loss
  • Reduces appetite
  • Lowers blood pressure
  • Improves cardiovascular outcomes

Evidence summary

Numerous human trials have demonstrated liraglutide's efficacy in improving glycemic control and promoting weight loss in individuals with type 2 diabetes and obesity. These studies consistently show significant benefits, though long-term safety data is still being gathered.
