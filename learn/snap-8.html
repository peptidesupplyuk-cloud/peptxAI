Overview

Neuropeptide that modulates SNARE complex formation, reducing muscle contraction depth. Topical alternative to botulinum toxin for expression lines.

How it works

Snap-8 is a peptide that interferes with the SNARE complex, a group of proteins responsible for muscle contraction. By inhibiting this complex, Snap-8 reduces the depth of muscle contractions, particularly in facial muscles. This action mimics the effect of botulinum toxin but is applied topically. The peptide works by modulating neurotransmitter release, leading to a temporary reduction in muscle activity.

What the research describes

  • Reduces the appearance of expression lines
  • Decreases muscle contraction depth
  • Provides a non-invasive alternative to botulinum toxin
  • Improves skin smoothness
  • May enhance skin hydration

Evidence summary

There are limited human trials specifically evaluating Snap-8. Some small-scale studies suggest it can reduce wrinkle depth, but these are often manufacturer-sponsored. More independent, large-scale clinical trials are needed to confirm efficacy and safety.

Evidence grade C — Early research.
