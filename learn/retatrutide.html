Overview

First-in-class triple agonist targeting GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon receptors. Phase III data shows ~28.7% body weight reduction. FDA submission expected 2027. Oral formulation in early development.

How it works

Retatrutide works by activating three receptors: GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon. This activation enhances insulin secretion, reduces appetite, and increases energy expenditure. The combined effect leads to significant weight loss and improved metabolic health. It also influences glucose metabolism and lipid profiles.

What the research describes

  • Enhanced insulin sensitivity
  • Reduced appetite

Evidence summary

There are a few human trials, primarily in Phase III, showing significant weight loss and metabolic improvements. However, long-term safety and efficacy data are still needed. The trials indicate promising results but are limited in duration and participant diversity.

Evidence grade B, Emerging.
