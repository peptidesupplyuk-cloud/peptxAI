Overview

TREK-1 channel blocker with rapid antidepressant and pro-cognitive effects. Research suggests faster onset than traditional antidepressants.

How it works

PE-22-28 works by blocking the TREK-1 potassium channels, which are involved in regulating neuronal excitability and mood. By inhibiting these channels, PE-22-28 may enhance neurotransmitter release, particularly serotonin, leading to rapid antidepressant effects. This action can also improve cognitive function by modulating synaptic plasticity.

What the research describes

  • Rapid antidepressant effects
  • Improved cognitive function
  • Enhanced mood stabilization
  • Increased synaptic plasticity
  • Potential anxiolytic effects

Evidence summary

There are currently no published human clinical trials for PE-22-28. Existing evidence is primarily from animal studies, which suggest rapid antidepressant and cognitive-enhancing effects. Human trials are needed to confirm efficacy and safety.

Evidence grade C, Early research.
