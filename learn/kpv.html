Overview

Potent anti-inflammatory peptide derived from alpha-MSH. Studied for gut health and inflammatory conditions.

How it works

KPV is a tripeptide derived from alpha-MSH that exerts its effects by binding to melanocortin receptors, particularly MC1R and MC3R. This interaction leads to the inhibition of pro-inflammatory cytokine production and the promotion of anti-inflammatory cytokines. It also reduces oxidative stress and modulates immune cell activity, contributing to its anti-inflammatory properties.

What the research describes

  • Reduces inflammation in the gut
  • Decreases systemic inflammatory markers
  • Promotes healing of intestinal mucosa
  • Modulates immune response
  • Reduces oxidative stress

Use areas

KPV is researched across multiple areas:

Longevity

KPV's anti-inflammatory properties may contribute to reduced chronic inflammation, a factor in ageing.

Health optimisation

KPV may support gut health and reduce systemic inflammation, contributing to overall health optimisation.

Evidence summary

There are currently no published human clinical trials specifically investigating KPV. Most evidence comes from animal studies and in vitro experiments showing anti-inflammatory effects. Human trials are needed to confirm efficacy and safety in clinical settings.

Evidence grade C — Early research.
