Overview

Critical coenzyme involved in cellular energy production, DNA repair, and metabolic function. Declines significantly with age.

How it works

NAD+ acts as a coenzyme in redox reactions, transferring electrons from one reaction to another, which is crucial for ATP production in the mitochondria. It also plays a role in DNA repair by activating sirtuins, a family of proteins involved in cellular stress responses. Additionally, NAD+ is involved in the regulation of circadian rhythms and metabolic pathways.

What the research describes

  • Enhances mitochondrial function and energy production
  • Supports DNA repair and genomic stability
  • Improves metabolic health and insulin sensitivity
  • May enhance cognitive function and neuroprotection
  • Potentially extends lifespan in animal models

Use areas

NAD+ is researched across multiple areas:

Longevity

NAD+ is relevant to longevity due to its role in DNA repair and mitochondrial function, which are critical for healthy aging.

Fitness

NAD+ may enhance athletic performance by improving mitochondrial efficiency and energy production.

Health optimisation

NAD+ supports overall health by enhancing metabolic function and protecting against age-related decline.

Evidence summary

There are several human trials examining NAD+ precursors like nicotinamide riboside and nicotinamide mononucleotide, showing improvements in metabolic health and markers of aging. However, direct evidence on NAD+ itself is limited, and long-term effects are not well-studied.

Evidence grade B — Emerging.
