Overview

Naturally occurring peptide used clinically in over 30 countries for immune modulation. Approved for hepatitis B and C in some regions.

How it works

Thymosin Alpha-1 enhances the immune response by promoting the maturation of T-cells, which are crucial for adaptive immunity. It increases the production of cytokines, which help in coordinating the immune response. Additionally, it modulates the activity of dendritic cells, enhancing their ability to present antigens and activate T-cells.

What the research describes

  • Enhances T-cell production and function
  • Increases cytokine production
  • Improves immune response against viral infections
  • Reduces inflammation
  • Promotes dendritic cell activity

Evidence summary

There are several human clinical trials demonstrating Thymosin Alpha-1's efficacy in enhancing immune function, particularly in viral infections like hepatitis B and C. However, more large-scale, randomized controlled trials are needed to confirm these findings and explore other potential applications.

Evidence grade B — Emerging.
