Calculator FAQ

How do I work out units to draw?+

Concentration = vial mg ÷ water mL. Volume per dose = dose ÷ concentration. On a U-100 insulin syringe, 100 units = 1 mL, so units = volume (mL) × 100. The calculator does all of this and pre-fills typical values per compound from our knowledge base.

What is "per unit on the syringe"?+

It's how many mcg sit in a single unit mark on your U-100 syringe at your chosen dilution, handy for quickly eyeballing or adjusting a dose without recalculating.

What does the plasma-level model show?+

A simplified one-compartment model: each dose adds to the amount in your system, which then decays by the compound's half-life. Repeated dosing accumulates toward a steady state. It's relative and illustrative, for understanding accumulation and timing, not a prescription.

Is this medical advice?+

No. PeptX.AI tools are for laboratory and research use and education only. Nothing here recommends a compound, dose or protocol. Consult a qualified professional for anything affecting health.
